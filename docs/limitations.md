> Honest scope boundaries for the current `main` build. Every claim below

## L1 — Validation signal is statistically thin

<!-- generated by: scripts/11_validate.py at d500c8499e35c18c81d48a3f765bdaa93c80a235 ; input exposure_20260617T035233Z.parquet -->

The pilot AOI (Sever do Vouga, ~30×30 km) contains **3,045 scored assets** of
which **5 were intersected by a 2025 ICNF Áreas Ardidas perimeter** — a base
rate of 0.16%. At that count, a single asset moving between deciles swings
top-decile lift by ±2.00×. The mandatory ablation (burn-history features
removed) **ties** the full configuration (top-decile lift 2.00× vs 0.00×;
Spearman ρ 0.0379 vs 0.0371), which means **this run does not resolve which
features carry the signal**.

Lift and Spearman become interpretable once N(burned assets) reaches the
dozens. **Pillar 2 of the operationalization program** (`docs/operationalization.md`
§3) addresses this by running the pipeline on four additional mainland-PT AOIs
(Pedrógão, Serra da Estrela, Peneda-Gerês, Monchique) chosen specifically for
higher burn density, widening the validated pool to the dozens of burned assets.

Until then, the defensible claim is:
*"A transparent, reproducible prioritization screen validated against
subsequent burns — nothing stronger."*

## L2 — The validated rank is structural-only; live fire-weather is unweighted by design

The validated score (`config/exposure_score.yaml` v0.3.1) is a linear
combination of **six structural features**. FWI was restored with a 0.10 weight
in v0.3.0, but v0.3.1 returns it to weight 0 after Wave-2 multi-AOI validation
showed the backdated FWI did not earn its weight (`scripts/24_wave2_validate.py`;
the FWI and topology features stay computed and reported but unweighted, so the
six structural weights renormalise back to the v0.2.0 values). Two time-varying
features are therefore absent from the validated structural run:

- **`fwi_p95_recent_season`** was dropped project-wide in WU-6: no verified,
  public, programmatic FWI source was found in-session (IPMA has no public REST
  API; Copernicus CDS requires an account). The v0.2.0 changelog records the
  decision and the renormalisation (six weights ÷ 0.90). See
  `config/exposure_score.yaml`.

- **`recent_burn_share_12mo`** is correctly **nulled** in the backdated
  validation window (2024-12-31 cutoff) to avoid temporal leakage: the
  Prithvi burn-scar composite spans the *recent* season, which does not overlap
  a backdated scoring window without leaking post-window observations. The guard
  in `src/wildfire_exposure_eo/features.py` logs the rationale; the validation
  report confirms the feature is absent from the evaluated run.

The README's headline asks "…most exposed **this season**?". A live current-season
fire-weather signal (EWDS Copernicus FWI, `fwi_fwi_current`) was wired in at
v0.3.0, but Wave-2 validation (`scripts/24_wave2_validate.py`,
`docs/validation_report.md`) showed the backdated FWI weight did **not** improve
burn discrimination, so v0.3.1 returns it to weight 0. The **validated structural
rank therefore carries no live fire-weather weight by design** — the FWI signal
is available-but-unweighted and is intended for an *operational overlay* (validated
structural rank × current fire weather), not for the validated structural score
itself. Treat the "this season" framing as applying to that operational overlay,
not to the structural validation reported here.

## L3 — Scores are AOI-relative, not cross-AOI comparable

Each feature is percentile-ranked **within the evaluated AOI** before weighting
(methodology §11). An exposure score of 0.90 means "in the top 10% of this
AOI's distribution" — it does **not** mean the asset is in the top 10% of any
national distribution. Two assets from different AOIs cannot be ranked against
each other without a national reference distribution.

This is a documented scope boundary, not a defect. It becomes acute when Pillar
2 produces results across four AOIs simultaneously, and is a hard prerequisite
for Pillar 3 (operationalization) which targets *absolute* actionable
thresholds: those thresholds require either (a) raw pre-percentile feature
space thresholds or (b) a national reference distribution. Pillar 3
(`prompts/20`) must resolve this design decision.

## L4 — Fuel-map scale mismatch

<!-- generated by: scripts/11_validate.py at d500c8499e35c18c81d48a3f765bdaa93c80a235 -->

The fuel layer's EFFIS source (vintage 2023) has an effective native resolution
of **250 m**, resampled onto the 10 m working grid and then refined by DGT COSc
land cover at 10 m. EFFIS fuel classes are coarse relative to the 20–100 m
asset buffers used to extract per-asset features. The DGT COSc rule-based
refinement partially compensates but does not recover sub-250 m fuel variation.
Treat `fuel_class_severity_weight` as a coarse **contextual** input, not a
parcel-level fuel measurement.

This is plan caveat #3 in `docs/validation_report.md` and is reproduced
verbatim by `scripts/11_validate.py`.

## L5 — Burn-scar domain shift and the detection-not-forecast framing

The Prithvi-EO-2.0-300M-BurnScars checkpoint
(`ibm-nasa-geospatial/Prithvi-EO-2.0-300M-BurnScars` @ `a3f2c410…`) was
trained on HLS (Harmonized Landsat and Sentinel-2) scenes from North American
fires. The WU-10 domain-shift sanity check (`scripts/09_burn_scar_sanity.py`)
measured transfer quality on three 2025 Portuguese ICNF fires: mean IoU 0.67–0.75,
recall 0.98–1.00 — adequate for a screening rank input, but calibration
differences from the training distribution remain. The de-grid p85 composite
(run `20260615T192025Z`) achieves detection F1 **0.644** @threshold 0.30,
precision **0.81** @threshold 0.50 vs the 2023–2025 ICNF perimeters.

**This score detects burn *scars* — visible post-event spectral signatures of
fires that already happened.** It is not ignition prediction and it is not a
forecast. The `recent_burn_share_12mo` feature uses the scar composite as a
contextual input (assets near recent burns rank higher on this feature), not as
a forward-looking fire-danger signal.

The multi-year detection validation is emitted by
`scripts/16_burn_scar_multiyear_validate.py`.

## L6 — Rank-not-probability; demonstrator-not-service

The exposure score is a **relative rank** — it answers "where does this asset
sit in the AOI's distribution?" It is not a calibrated probability of future
fire, not a risk probability, not a chance of fire reaching the asset. Reporting
it as a probability would violate CLAUDE.md non-negotiable #6.

This repo is a **civic-tech demonstrator**: any município, civil-protection
office, or researcher can re-run it on a fresh clone and get the same artifacts
for their own district. It is not production-ready, not utility-grade, and not
operationally validated at a service level. Words like "production-ready",
"utility-grade", or "operationally validated" are forbidden in this codebase
(CLAUDE.md non-negotiable #9).
